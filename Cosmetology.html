Laser hair removal

Removal of unwanted hair using laser irradiation.

Massage

How It Works: The laser targets the melanin in the hair follicle, which absorbs the light and converts it into heat. The heat damages the hair follicle, inhibiting its ability to produce new hair.

Suitability: Laser hair removal is most effective for individuals with dark hair and light skin because the laser can target the dark pigment more easily. Advances in technology have made it possible for individuals with darker skin tones to undergo laser hair removal, although results may vary.

Facial cleansing

A procedure aimed at cleansing the skin, eliminating acne and comedones.

Facial care

Cleansing Techniques: Manual Cleansing: This involves using cleansers, exfoliants, and facial brushes or sponges to manually remove impurities from the skin's surface. Chemical Exfoliation: This method uses acids like alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) or beta hydroxy acids (BHAs) to dissolve dead skin cells and unclog pores.

Professional vs. Home Facial Cleansing: Professional Facials: Conducted by licensed estheticians, professional facials often involve advanced techniques and specialized products tailored to individual skin needs. At-Home Care: Daily cleansing routines at home involve using suitable cleansers, exfoliants, and masks based on skin type and concerns.

Botox and fillers

Using injections to reduce wrinkles and improve facial contours.

Manicure and pedicure

Botox (Botulinum Toxin): How It Works: Botox is a neurotoxin that temporarily paralyzes or relaxes muscles, preventing them from contracting. This reduces the appearance of wrinkles caused by repetitive muscle movements, such as frown lines and crow's feet.

Dermal Fillers: Types of Fillers: Dermal fillers are substances injected beneath the skin to add volume, fill lines, and enhance facial contours. Common types include hyaluronic acid fillers, collagen fillers, and others.


Microdermabrasion

A mechanical exfoliation method that helps improve skin texture.

Hydrotherapy

Procedure Overview: Mechanical Exfoliation: Microdermabrasion involves the use of a machine that emits fine crystals or a diamond-tipped wand to gently abrade the outer layer of the skin. Vacuum Suction: Simultaneously, a vacuum suction device removes the exfoliated skin cells, stimulating blood flow and promoting collagen production.

Purpose and Benefits: Exfoliation: Microdermabrasion exfoliates the outermost layer of dead skin cells, promoting cell turnover and revealing fresher, healthier skin. Improving Skin Texture: It helps address concerns such as rough skin texture, uneven pigmentation, and mild acne scars.

Mesotherapy

Introduction of special cocktails into the skin for hydration, nutrition and rejuvenation.

Aromatherapy

Purpose of Mesotherapy: Hydration: Mesotherapy helps improve skin hydration, making it useful for individuals with dry or dehydrated skin. Nutrition: The infusion of vitamins and minerals provides essential nutrients directly to the skin, promoting overall skin health. Rejuvenation: Mesotherapy is often used for facial rejuvenation, reducing the appearance of fine lines and promoting a more youthful complexion.

Peels

Chemical peels are designed to remove the top layers of skin to improve its appearance and texture.

Wraps

Types of Chemical Peels: Superficial Peels: These peels use mild acids, such as alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) or beta hydroxy acids (BHAs), to exfoliate the outer layer of the skin. They are suitable for addressing minor skin concerns and require little to no downtime. Medium Peels: Medium-depth peels typically use stronger acids, such as trichloroacetic acid (TCA), to penetrate deeper into the skin. They are effective in treating more pronounced issues like fine lines, pigmentation, and acne scars. Deep Peels: Deep peels, often using phenol, penetrate the deepest layers of the skin. They are reserved for more severe skin concerns and are usually performed under sedation or general anesthesia.
